Mental Health Trends in Tech: How Companies can Help

Kaylynn Crawford 2016

Visualizations and analysis on the prevalence of mental health issues, ease of access to resources, and company practices that encourage good mental health.
Data visualization and machine learning project for SDS 136 and SDS 293. Smith College 2016.

Prevalence of Mental Health Disorders in Tech

  • 40% of survey respondants have a mental disorder
  • 71% who responded said that their mental disorder affects their productivity

575 people out of the 1433 who answered this question say they currently having a mental disorder. This is 40% of the survey population. All of these people are employed, most at technology companies. Of the 287 people who answered the next question, 204 say that their mental disorder affects their productivity. Obviously, this is something that companies should address, for both altruistic reasons and practical ones.

Role of Tech Companies in Mental Health Issues

Companies can play either a positive or a negative role in the mental health of their employees. For example, by providing insurance that covers mental health issues as well as physical health issues, companies can encourage employees to get treatment if necessary.
On the other hand, companies that discourage taking days off for mental health issues, discourage communication, and don't provide mental health benefits can have a detrimental affect on the mental health of their employees.
As you can see from the graphs, under half of survey respondents knew that their insurance covered mental health benefits, and responses were very mixed regarding whether respondents felt that they could take time off without repercussions.
